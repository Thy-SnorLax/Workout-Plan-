💊 Supplement Protocol — Creatine + Protein

You have the two supplements that actually matter for body composition. Everything else is optional. Here's how to use them to maximise the plan.

🔋 Creatine Monohydrate
→5g every single day — training AND rest days. Consistency saturates the muscle; skipping days lets levels drop.
→No loading phase needed. Straight 5g/day fully saturates in 3–4 weeks. Timing doesn't matter — stir it into your post-workout shake out of habit.
→Expect +1–3 lbs scale weight in week 1–2. That's water pulled into the muscle (makes you look fuller), NOT fat. Don't panic on the Progress tab — judge fat loss by the waist measurement and photos instead.
→Drink more water. Creatine increases intramuscular water demand — aim for 3+ litres a day, especially while cutting.
→What it does: more reps before failure, faster strength recovery between sets, slightly better muscle retention in a deficit. It's the most studied, most effective legal supplement there is.
🥤 Protein Powder
→Use it to hit 185g, not to replace whole-food meals. Food keeps you fuller on a cut — powder fills the gap when you're short for the day.
→Post-workout scoop = convenient protein + the carrier for your creatine. One shake, both supplements.
→A scoop before bed (if short on protein) helps overnight muscle repair — slow-digesting if it's casein, but whey is fine too.

⏱️ Nutrient Timing — Squeeze Out Extra Gains

Total daily calories and protein drive 90% of results. But timing the remaining details well gives you a real edge on a cut where every bit of muscle retention counts.

1Carbs around training. Put the bulk of your 160g carbs in the meal before and the meal after your workout — fuels performance and refills muscle glycogen where it matters.
2Protein every 3–4 hours. Spread your 185g across 4–5 feedings (~40g each) rather than two big hits. Keeps muscle protein synthesis elevated all day.
3Pre-workout meal: protein + carbs 1–2 hours before lifting. Even a banana + shake beats training fasted for strength.
4Post-workout shake: whey + creatine within an hour. The "anabolic window" is wider than people claim, but on a cut it's an easy win to just do it.
5Sleep is the real multiplier. 7–9 hours. Creatine and protein do nothing if you're under-recovering — most muscle repair and fat-loss hormone regulation happens during deep sleep.

🟣 Bowflex PR1000 — Exercise Reference

→Lat Pulldown: Attach overhead bar. Sit facing away, pull bar to upper chest driving elbows down into pockets. Max rod count for heaviest sets.
→Seated Cable Row: Use the low-cable position. Sit on the floor or bench facing the machine, pull handles to lower ribs, hold 2 sec.
→Chest Press (Cable): Attach handles at chest height. Stand or sit facing away, press forward and cross hands slightly at lockout for peak contraction.
→Tricep Pushdown: High cable attachment, rope or bar. Pin elbows to sides, extend fully and hold 1 sec.
→Face Pull: High cable, rope handle. Pull toward forehead, elbows flared wide and high. Rear delts + external rotation.
→Leg Curl: Use the leg attachment. Seated or lying position — seated version loads hamstring in a stretch for more growth. Curl, hold 1 sec peak, lower in 3.
→Leg Press (Belt): Attach the leg press belt from the lower cable. Drive feet forward against the resistance, full extension, slow return.
→Bicep Curl (Cable): Low cable, straight bar. Stand back slightly so the cable pulls your arms forward — keeps tension at the top unlike free-weight curls.
→Rod resistance: Each rod adds ~5 lbs. Engage rods from the outside in. Never jerk the connection — clip firmly before releasing.

🔴 Training To Failure — Rules

1Failure = the point where you physically cannot complete another full rep with good form. Not "it's burning" — actual failure.
2Reserve failure for the LAST set of an exercise unless noted otherwise. Earlier sets stay at RIR 1–2 (1–2 reps shy of failure).
3Never go to failure on the big compound barbell lifts (squat, deadlift, bench) — too risky. Failure is for machines, cables, and isolation moves.
4Once you can get the top rep count for 2 sets in a row, add 5 lbs next session.

⚡ Progressive Overload Rules

1Add weight before reps. If the rep range is 8–12, load so you hit 8 with good form. Once you hit 12 clean, add weight next session.
2Track your weights. Use the notes app or a notebook. Mindlessly repeating the same weight for weeks = no progress.
3Compound lifts first, always. Never do isolation before compound — you'll be pre-fatigued where it matters most.
4Rest periods matter. Shorter rest = more metabolic stress (good for fat loss). Longer rest = more strength recovered. Follow the listed times.

🟣 Variation Exercises — Why They're In Here

▸African (Drag) Curl — Targets the long head (bicep peak) by pulling elbows back. Different stimulus from standard curls.
▸Lean-Away Lateral Raise — Loads the side delt in the stretched position for more growth vs standard raises.
▸Deficit RDL — Standing on a small elevation extends the hamstring stretch range beyond standard RDL for more growth.
▸Heel-Elevated Goblet Squat — Shifts load to quads, allows deeper knee flexion, great stretch-biased finisher for quad detail.
▸Zottman Curl — Combines a supinated curl up with a pronated (reverse) curl down, hitting bicep AND brachioradialis/forearm in one move.
▸Incline Curl — Arms hang behind the body, creating a deep bicep stretch at the bottom — the most growth-efficient curl variation.
▸Meadows Row (1-arm) — Loaded stretch on the lat, extreme range of motion. Better back thickness builder than standard rows alone.
